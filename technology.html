How our system captures license-plate data

A step-by-step technical flow of detection, capture, validation, and delivery.

1 — Camera detection & capture

High-speed camera sensors mounted at the bus stop continuously monitor the approach lane. When an object enters the detection zone, the system triggers a short, high-resolution burst capture (multiple frames) to ensure at least one frame is free of motion blur. Exposure, shutter timing, and IR illumination are adjusted dynamically for day/night conditions.

Hardware: Global-shutter camera, IR illuminator, low-latency capture buffer.

1.

2 — Preprocessing & plate localization

Captured frames are run through a preprocessing pipeline (de-noise, contrast stretch, geometric correction). A lightweight object detection model isolates vehicle and plate regions, producing tight crops for downstream OCR. Multiple candidate crops are kept and scored by quality metrics (sharpness, occlusion, angle).

Software: Lightweight CNN detector, GPU/edge-accelerated preprocessing.

2.

3 — OCR & confidence scoring

Crops are sent to an OCR engine optimized for variable fonts and partial occlusion. Multiple hypotheses are generated and ranked by confidence. Post-processing applies regional plate format rules and fuzzy matching to increase accuracy and reduce false positives.

3.

4 — Validation, timestamping & delivery

Verified plates are timestamped, geo-tagged, and packaged with evidence frames. Secure delivery to enforcement endpoints occurs over TLS; optionally batched to meet local retention and privacy policies. Audit logs and chain-of-custody metadata are included for legal acceptability.
